Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish consisting of layers of flat pasta sheets alternated with various fillings, sauces, and cheeses, then baked to perfection. Traditionally, the layers include a rich meat-based sauce (ragù), a creamy béchamel sauce, and cheeses like mozzarella and Parmesan. Variations might use ricotta cheese, vegetables, or alternative protein sources.

The pasta sheets are typically boiled or oven-ready and provide structure to the dish, while the sauces add moisture and flavor. Once assembled in a casserole dish, lasagna is baked until the top is golden and bubbly, with slightly crispy edges. It is hearty, flavorful, and often served as a main course for family meals or special occasions.

Ingredients

  • ½ pound ground pork
  • ½ cup minced onion
  • ½ pound lean ground beef.
  • 1 (28 ounce) can crushed tomatoes
  • 1 (8 ounce) can tomato sauce
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley, divided
  • 1 clove garlic, crushed
  • 1 ½ teaspoons dried basil
  • 1 ½ teaspoons salt
  • ½ teaspoon dried oregano
  • ⅛ teaspoon white sugar
  • 1 (16 ounce) package lasagna noodles
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 3 large eggs
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  1. Cook the meatCook the ground meat in a skillet until browned and crumbly. Add the onion and continue cooking until it's translucent. Stir in the canned tomato products, half of the parsley, garlic, basil, 1.5 teaspoons of salt, oregano, and sugar.
  2. Cook the noodles Boil the lasagna noodles in lightly salted water until they're al dente.
  3. Make the cheese layer Mix cottage cheese, Parmesan cheese, eggs, the remaining parsley, the remaining salt, and pepper in a bowl.
  4. Assemble the lasagna Layer the ingredients according to the recipe (starting with sauce and ending with mozzarella) until the lasagna is assembled.
  5. Bake the lasagna Cover with foil and bake in the preheated to 375 degrees oven for 50 minutes until the cheese is melted and the top is golden brown.
